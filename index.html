My Journey

From Anthropology to Pastry Arts - A Story of Passion and Transformation

The Beginning

After completing my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Anthropology, I embraced a fulfilling life as a homemaker. Everything was going well—until I discovered a newfound passion for pastries. What started as a simple interest in baking videos turned into hands-on experiments in my kitchen, and before I knew it, I found myself thriving as a home baker.

The Turning Point

But I wanted more than success at home—I wanted to learn from the best. That's when I enrolled in the 9-month Diploma Program in Pastry and Bakery at APCA Bangalore. Coming from a humble background, this experience was truly transformative. It was a leap into a world I had only dreamt of, and I soaked in every moment.

Professional Growth

The turning point came during my internship at the Trou aux Biches Beachcomber Golf Resort & Spa in Mauritius. There, I received real-world exposure, honed my techniques under expert chefs, and, most importantly, found the confidence to step into the professional culinary world.

Today

Nowadays, I create exquisite pastries that tell a story, combining the finest ingredients with artistic vision to deliver unforgettable culinary experiences. I stand proud as a pastry and bakery chef, turning my passion into a career—proof that it's never too late to start over and follow what truly sets your soul on fire.
